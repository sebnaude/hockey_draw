# spec-033 — Uniform soft+slack treatment of deviation-tolerant constraints

Originally "remove ClubVsClubAlignment slack + bye-spacing soft analogue." Expanded (convenor direction, 2026-05-24) to a full pass over every hard rule that *tolerates deviation*: each must carry (a) a hard ceiling on how far it may deviate (tunable by `--slack`) and (b) a soft penalty pushing toward the perfect outcome. The audit surfaced five constraints needing work; this spec does all five.

## Why

The `--slack`/soft system on `final-form` is uneven. The convenor's principle: **every hard rule that can tolerate some imperfection should have a hard deviation ceiling *and* a soft term pushing toward perfection; rules that genuinely cannot deviate (physical impossibilities) stay purely hard.** Measured against that, five gaps (all verified against the worktree this session):

1. **`ClubVsClubAlignment` slack is dead in the solver but live in the tester.** Production club-alignment is the spec-005 *stacked* cluster (`ClubVsClubStackedWeekends` + `ClubVsClubStackedCoLocation`, `groups={'core','club_alignment'}`); **neither stacked atom reads slack**. The only reader of `slack['ClubVsClubAlignment']` is the engine method `_club_alignment_hard`/`_club_alignment_soft` (`unified.py:630,678,846`), whose engine key has **no `groups=`** (`registry.py:333`) so it is never dispatched — the registry calls it "legacy parity-reference engine path" (`registry.py:344-345`). Yet the tester still applies the slack (`tester.py:2011,2066`). Net: `--slack ClubVsClubAlignment N` loosens the *checker* against a rule the *solver* enforces strictly. The convenor wants alignment to be a fixed hard rule with no slack.

2. **`BalancedByeSpacing` tolerates deviation but has NO normal-mode soft analogue, floors at the raw ideal, and is mis-grouped.** (a) No `_bye_spacing_soft` in `unified.py`; `apply_stage_2_soft` (`unified.py:760-785`) adds no bye penalty — a soft analogue exists ONLY for regen (`balanced_bye_spacing_regen_soft.py`, `regen_soft`, excluded from normal builds by `stages.py:243`). (b) Its base slack `bye_spacing_base_slack` defaults to **0** (`config/defaults.py:124`), so the hard floor `S = ideal_bye_gap(R,b)` sits at the raw ideal — e.g. a 2-bye team over 22 playable rounds forced ≥11 rounds apart — a real infeasibility risk (convenor: "if we hard-floor the ideal we will not get a draw"). (c) It is grouped `{core, critical_feasibility}` (`registry.py:181-189`), but bye spacing is a *distinct* concern from matchup spacing (a team may play 16 games over 20 playable rounds and have 4 byes — a different spacing dimension from its repeat-meeting spacing), so the convenor wants it peeled out of `core` into its own toggle, like spec-032 does for `EqualMatchUpSpacing`. **Bye-round counting must also exclude no-play weekends** — those are everyone's structural byes, not schedulable ones.

3. **`TeamConflict` is HARD but should be soft-only.** `_team_conflict` (`unified.py:441`, dispatched in `apply_stage_1_hard`) reads `data['team_conflicts']` and forbids two named teams sharing a `(week, day_slot)`. The convenor wants this to be a *preference*, not a feasibility blocker — a soft penalty per concurrent appearance, no hard component. Registry entry `registry.py:249`, `groups={'core_hard'}`, sev 2; tester `_check_team_conflict` (`tester.py:1898`); severity map `severity.py:73-74`.

4. **`ClubGameSpread` is lopsided — its field-concentration structure has a soft push but NO hard ceiling.** `_club_game_spread_hard` (`unified.py:1018-1090`) caps only per-field interior *holes* (contiguity). The number of distinct fields a club uses per day is governed *only* by the soft off-primary penalty (`unified.py:1116-1142`); nothing hard stops a club spreading across 3+ fields. The convenor wants ClubGameSpread to be **two interlocked structures, each hard-capped + soft-pushed**: contiguity (≤ hole-cap, push→0 holes — already present) AND field concentration (**hard ≤2 fields**, push→1 — hard cap MISSING). (`ClubDaySameField` forces 1 field but only on a club's designated club day, not every day.)

5. **`ClubNoConcurrentSlot` is HARD + capacity-aware but should be soft + slack.** `club_no_concurrent_slot.py` caps a club's games per `(club,week,day,location)` slot at the capacity-aware `ceil(n_loc/S)` — it *permits* forced double-ups when a club has more games at a venue than slots. The convenor wants the opposite stance: a hard ceiling of **1 overlap per slot (+slack)** and a soft penalty pushing overlaps → 0 — i.e. when a club's day spans two fields at a venue, stagger them across slots where possible rather than overlapping. Registry `registry.py:397` `groups={'core','critical_feasibility','core_hard'}` sev 2; tester `_check_club_no_concurrent_slot` (`tester.py:2175`); severity map `severity.py:69`.

Cost of not solving: a misleading tester (1,3,5), uneven bye spread with no optimisation pressure (2), unbounded field fragmentation for a club's day (4), and forced concurrent club games the convenor would rather stagger (5).
